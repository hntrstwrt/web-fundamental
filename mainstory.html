The Friday the 13th I'll Always Remember

It was the end of spring semester and I was all packed up and ready to fly back to my hometown on the west coast; all that was left was getting to the airport and boarding the plane. My flight boarded at 6:30pm out of Newark Airport, so I decided to leave my dorm in the East Village at 3:00pm. Usually, I'd only give myself about an hour and a half of slack time, but I thought this time I'd be extra prepared. I got in my Uber at 3:00pm and briefly, everything felt fine.

But here's the thing:

  • I woke up feeling like something was about to happen, I wasn't sure what...but I could feel it.
  • Every time I have to fly I already feel like something terrible is about to happen.
  • I had like 300 pounds of luggage with me so there was no way I was about to take the train.
  • Uber, which I already know is a scam, really took advantage on that day.
  • Traffic, of all days, decided that day was the day it was going to have me really messed up.
